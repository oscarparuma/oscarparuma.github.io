SoftCioTec

Free/Libre and Open-Source Software (FLOSS) Evolution: Analysis and Visualization of Socio-Technical Aspects

Incorporating social aspects in the field of Software Engineering (Social Software Engineering - SSE) and their relationship with technical aspects related to the software development process have generated a growing interest in the scientific community that studies the software evolution. That is why this research project is focused on analyzing data obtained from multiple information sources related to Free/Libre and Open-Source Software (FLOSS) projects that represents clearly the most relevant aspects in each of the development process stages. Our aim is to identify the correlation between software evolution and the evolution of the underlying social network and to explore visualization metaphors to help the identification and analysis of these relationships.

The research problem is framed by the identification and visualization of relationships among different aspects of the software evolution (developers, issues, source code, commit comments, source code documentation, software structure/architecture, mailing lists) and the interaction between members of the underlying social network.

The main research questions to be answered are:

  1. From the analysis of the data stored in FLOSS repositories, ¿What are the relationships that can be identified between the socio-technical aspects related to software evolution?
  2. As software evolves, ¿How can be characterized and make evident the evolution of the community formed by different actors involved in the development process?
  3. ¿What are the more relevant characteristics that emerge when representing graphically the relationship between software quality and community dynamics (evolution and interaction) formed by different actors involved in the development process?
  4. ¿What are the most suitable metaphors for representing multimodal data that reflect the evolution of FLOSS communities and their products?
